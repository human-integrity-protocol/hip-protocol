1. Service Description

The Human Integrity Protocol ("HIP") is an open verification protocol. The hipprotocol.org website and the protocol-layer Worker (collectively, the "Service") provide:

  • Verification: Read endpoints (such as /api/proof/{hash}, /api/series/{id}, /api/collection/{id}) that allow any party to verify attestation records, series, and collections registered to the protocol.
  • Credential issuance — Tier 2 (peer vouch) and Tier 3 (biometric presence): Self-service credentialing endpoints under Charter Deployment Principle 5 (Permissionless Proliferation) and Charter Deployment Principle 7 (Zero Institutional Cost). These tiers are free, browser-side, and do not require payment or account creation. Tier 1 credentialing is performed by independent pathway operators (such as hipverify.org) and is not provided by hipprotocol.org directly.
  • Credential-signed write endpoints: Endpoints (such as /register-proof, /dispute-proof, series and collection mutation endpoints) that accept attestation submissions signed by a HIP credential. The protocol verifies the signature against the credential's public key before accepting the write.
  • Client-side tools: The hipprotocol.org website hosts in-browser tools for computing content hashes, signing attestations, and verifying signatures. These tools operate entirely in your browser; nothing about your local files leaves your device unless you explicitly publish an attestation.

Per Charter Deployment Principle 7 (Zero Institutional Cost), the protocol layer collects no fees. Per Charter Deployment Principle 8 (Protocol, Not Entity), hipprotocol.org is an institutional implementation of the protocol; it is not the protocol itself. For the data-handling side of your relationship with hipprotocol.org (separate from these contractual terms), see the HIP Protocol Privacy Policy.

2. Eligibility

The verification (read) endpoints are open to any party with a browser or HTTP client. The credential issuance and credential-signed write endpoints are open to any human capable of holding a HIP credential. Tier 1 credentialing through pathway operators (such as hipverify.org) imposes its own eligibility (typically age 13+ and possession of a valid government-issued ID); Tier 2 and Tier 3 credentialing through hipprotocol.org has no formal age requirement.

3. Verification of Attestations

The protocol's verification guarantee is structural: attestation signatures are verifiable by any party against canonical JSON serialization (RFC 8785 / JCS) using the public key on the credential. The Service makes the verification process available through both server-side endpoints and in-browser tools, but the cryptographic verification itself does not depend on the Service. Once an attestation is published, anyone with the content hash and public key can verify it independently — including after a hipprotocol.org sunset (see Privacy Policy § 12).

The Service does not warrant that any particular attestation reflects accurate facts about the world. The protocol records that a credential signed a content hash at a point in time; it does not validate the content's truthfulness, completeness, or fitness for any specific use. Verification of an attestation is a verification of cryptographic provenance, not a verification of the underlying claim's accuracy.

4. Submitting Attestations

Submitting an attestation through a credential-signed write endpoint is your assertion that you are the holder of the credential signing the attestation, and that the content hash you are signing represents content you intend to attest to. You are responsible for the contents of any data you publish under your credential.

Once submitted, attestations are stored on hipprotocol.org infrastructure and are eligible for ledger anchoring (see Privacy Policy § 9). Public ledger systems (OpenTimestamps over Bitcoin, IPFS, Arweave) are typically immutable by design — once anchored, attestation hashes and signatures may persist independently of hipprotocol.org's operational status. Do not submit attestations against personal data, secrets, or other content you do not want to be subject to long-lived public verifiability.

10. Sunset and Continuity

The HIP protocol's verification surface is intentionally designed to outlast any single operator. Charter Deployment Principle 8 (Protocol, Not Entity) reinforces this: hipprotocol.org is an institutional implementation, not the protocol itself.

If hipprotocol.org's operations are wound down, attestations previously registered through hipprotocol.org continue to be cryptographically verifiable against their public keys regardless of hipprotocol.org's operational status. Once the protocol's Steward Node tier is activated, the records necessary for ongoing protocol-layer verification can be migrated to Steward Nodes. Until that tier is activated, hipprotocol.org will publish a final read-only snapshot of the necessary protocol-facing fields to a public archive before any wind-down completes.

A wind-down or operator-transfer notice will appear at hipprotocol.org and on the HIP Protocol GitHub repository at least thirty (30) days before any non-reversible step is taken. Any updated terms that apply under a successor operator will be published here. A pathway operator (such as hipverify.org) sunset is governed by that operator's own terms and does not retract attestations previously made under credentials issued by that pathway.
